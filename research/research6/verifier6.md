## Final Synthesized Answer

**The family has 5 children.**

### Reasoning:
1. The girl mentioned in the problem has 3 brothers, establishing that there are 3 boys in the family.

2. Each of these brothers has 2 sisters. Since all brothers belong to the same family, they share the same sisters. This means there are exactly 2 girls in the family.

3. The original girl mentioned is one of these 2 sisters (she and one other sister).

4. Total children: 3 boys + 2 girls = **5 children**

### Verification:
From any brother's perspective:
- He has 2 brothers (the other 2 boys)
- He has 2 sisters (the 2 girls)
- This matches the problem statement that "each brother has 2 sisters"

The key insight is recognizing that "each brother has 2 sisters" does not mean each brother has different sisters - they all share the same 2 sisters, and the original girl is one of those sisters.
