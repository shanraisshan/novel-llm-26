This is a classic riddle. Let me think about what starts short and becomes tall over time.

1. "Short when young" means the object is small or low at the beginning of its life.
2. "Tall when old" means it grows taller as it ages.

Let me reconsider. The riddle says short when young, tall when old. What starts small and grows tall?

- A tree: starts as a small seedling and grows tall over many years. That fits.
- A person: humans grow taller as they age (at least from childhood to adulthood). That fits too.
- A shadow: shadows are short at midday and long in the evening. If we think of morning as "young" (early in the day) and evening as "old" (late in the day), shadows grow longer. But shadows are actually long in the morning too.

Wait -- I need to reconsider the candle answer. A candle is tall when new and short when old (burned down). That is the reverse of this riddle.

This riddle as stated -- short when young, tall when old -- most naturally fits a tree or a person. But actually, thinking more carefully, this riddle is sometimes posed in the reverse form (tall when young, short when old = candle). The version here says short when young and tall when old, which points to something that grows.

The most common answer given for this exact phrasing is actually a **candle** -- but that does not logically fit since candles shrink. Many people default to "candle" because they conflate this with the more famous version of the riddle.

Thinking purely logically: short when young, tall when old = something that grows over time. A tree, a person, or a plant.

I will go with a **tree** as my answer, since it is the clearest example of something that is short when young and tall when old.

## Final Answer
A tree. A tree starts as a small seedling (short when young) and grows to be tall over many years (tall when old).
