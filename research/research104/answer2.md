This is a classic riddle. Let me think about what starts short and becomes tall over time:

1. "Short when young" - something that is small or low at the beginning of its life.
2. "Tall when old" - something that grows taller as it ages.

The most common and well-known answer to this riddle is a candle. Wait, let me reconsider. A candle is tall when new and short when old (it melts down). That is the opposite of what the riddle describes.

Let me reconsider what starts short and grows tall:
- A tree starts as a small seedling and grows tall over many years.
- A person starts short as a child and grows tall as an adult.
- A shadow can be short at midday and long/tall in the evening.

But thinking about this as a classic riddle, the most fitting answer is a candle -- except the riddle says short when young and tall when old, which is the inverse of a candle.

However, some people also answer "a shadow" -- shadows are short during midday (when the sun is "young" in the day) and tall in the evening (when the day is "old").

I will go with a candle, since this riddle is most traditionally associated with that answer, and many versions of it circulate with slightly different phrasing. But the literal phrasing here better fits a tree or a person.

I will go with my straightforward interpretation: a tree.

## Final Answer
A tree. A tree starts as a short seedling when it is young and grows tall as it ages over the years.
