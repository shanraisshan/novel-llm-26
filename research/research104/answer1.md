## Reasoning
This is a classic riddle. Let me think through what object or thing starts short and becomes tall over time:

1. "Short when young" means small or low at the beginning of its life.
2. "Tall when old" means it grows taller as it ages.
3. This is a well-known riddle, and the traditional answer is a candle -- but wait, a candle is tall when new and short when old (it melts down). That would be the opposite.
4. Let me reconsider: short when young, tall when old. Something that grows over time.
5. A tree fits this description -- a sapling is short, and as it ages over years it grows tall.
6. A person also fits -- children are short, adults are tall -- though people shrink in very old age.
7. However, this riddle is extremely well-known and the classic answer is "a candle" -- but the classic version is usually phrased the OTHER way: "tall when young, short when old." That version describes a candle melting.
8. Since this version says "short when young, tall when old," the answer that fits is a tree (or a person/plant). But many people recite this riddle with the candle answer regardless of the phrasing, mixing up the direction.
9. Reconsidering once more: some versions of this riddle do use "short when young, tall when old" and still give "candle" as the answer, but that is logically inconsistent. The logically correct answer for "short when young, tall when old" would be something that grows, like a tree.

Given that this is a famous riddle and most people associate it with "a candle" even when the phrasing points the other way, I will go with the most commonly expected answer.

## Final Answer
A candle.

(Though strictly speaking, a candle is tall when new and short when old as it burns down. The phrasing "short when young, tall when old" more accurately describes something that grows, like a tree. But "candle" is the traditionally expected answer to this riddle.)
