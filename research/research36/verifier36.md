## Final Synthesized Answer

**The smallest number is 3.11.**

When comparing decimal numbers, we must compare the actual numerical values, not the apparent length or digit patterns of the decimal portion. Converting all numbers to the same number of decimal places (hundredths) clarifies the comparison:

- 3.9 = 3.90 (3 and 90 hundredths)
- 3.11 = 3.11 (3 and 11 hundredths)
- 3.7 = 3.70 (3 and 70 hundredths)

Since all three numbers share the same whole number part (3), we compare only the decimal portions:
- 0.11 (eleven hundredths) < 0.70 (seventy hundredths) < 0.90 (ninety hundredths)

Therefore, the complete ordering from smallest to largest is:
**3.11 < 3.7 < 3.9**

This can be understood intuitively by thinking in terms of money: 11 cents is clearly less than 70 cents, which is less than 90 cents. A common error would be to compare "11" and "7" as if they were integers (where 7 < 11), but in decimal notation, 0.11 represents eleven hundredths while 0.7 represents seventy hundredths.
