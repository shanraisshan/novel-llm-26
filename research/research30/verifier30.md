# Verifier Analysis

## Original Question
"Is your answer to this question exactly one syllable long? Answer yes or no."

- **Unique Insights**: Clearly worked through the paradox and self-corrected from an initial "No" to "Yes".

- **Unique Insights**: This answer demonstrates classic scope confusion - interpreting "answer" as the entire response rather than the yes/no response to the question.

   - By this logic, one could make any answer "correct" by adding more text

## Final Synthesized Answer

**Yes**

This is a self-referential question, but NOT a paradox. The key insight is:

1. Both "yes" and "no" are exactly one syllable long
2. The question asks whether the answer is exactly one syllable long
3. If you answer "yes" - you are claiming your answer IS one syllable, and "yes" IS one syllable = TRUE and CONSISTENT
4. If you answer "no" - you are claiming your answer is NOT one syllable, but "no" IS one syllable = FALSE and INCONSISTENT

Therefore, "Yes" is the only self-consistent answer. The answer "yes" truthfully describes itself as being one syllable long.

The scope confusion exhibited by Answer 5 (treating the entire response as "the answer") is incorrect because the question explicitly constrains the answer to "yes or no." The explanatory text is not part of the answer being evaluated.
