"If you turn the word 'SAIL' upside down, what do you see?"

**Expected Answer (per research)**: LIES

## Summary of Each Answer

### Answer 1
- **Core Conclusion**: Turning "SAIL" upside down produces "TIVS" or something similar that does not form a recognizable English word.
- **Key Evidence**: Analyzes each letter individually - S stays S (rotational symmetry), A becomes inverted V shape, I stays I, L becomes reversed L or 7-like shape. States the word would read right-to-left as inverted forms of L-I-A-S.
- **Unique Insights**: Notes that if literally flipped, you might see "TIVS" but this is not meaningful.

### Answer 2

- **Key Evidence**: Similar letter-by-letter analysis. Contrasts with words like "NOON" or "SOS" which have rotational symmetry.
- **Unique Insights**: Notes S could resemble a "2" when rotated.

### Answer 3
- **Core Conclusion**: Turning "SAIL" upside down produces "TIVS" which is not a recognizable English word.
- **Key Evidence**: Same letter-by-letter breakdown. Lists letters with rotational symmetry (H, I, N, O, S, X, Z) that could form coherent words.
- **Unique Insights**: Mentions that only letters with specific symmetry properties would create readable rotated words.

### Answer 4

- **Key Evidence**: Standard letter-by-letter analysis. Notes the distinction between 180-degree rotation vs. horizontal mirror image.
- **Unique Insights**: **This is the only answer that mentions "LIES"** - stating it would require horizontal flipping, not upside-down rotation.

### Answer 5
- **Core Conclusion**: Rotating "SAIL" 180 degrees produces "TIVS" or similar, not a recognizable word.

- **Unique Insights**: Explicitly states "LIES" is the common intended puzzle answer but clarifies it requires horizontal mirror, not 180-degree rotation.

## Critical Analysis: The Research Question vs. Reality

The research document claims that:
- SAIL rotated 180 degrees becomes LIES
- Sources like David Blackledge's blog list "sail/lies" as a symbiotogram pair
- The transformation involves a->e conversion

However, **all 5 independent answers conclude this is NOT correct for 180-degree rotation**. The answers explain that:
1. When rotated 180 degrees, 'A' does not become 'E' - it becomes an inverted A shape
2. When rotated 180 degrees, 'L' does not become 'L' readable - it becomes a rotated L shape
3. The word would read right-to-left as scrambled letters

**The key distinction**: The "sail/lies" ambigram works with **specific fonts and creative styling**, not with standard block capital letters. Natural ambigrams often require specially designed letterforms where the 'a' is styled to look like 'e' when inverted.

## Final Synthesized Answer

**When you turn the word "SAIL" upside down (rotate it 180 degrees), you do NOT see a recognizable English word.**

The result would appear as scrambled, unreadable letters - something approximating "TIVS" or the inverted forms of L-I-A-S read in reverse order.

**Why "LIES" is not the correct answer for standard text:**
While "sail/lies" is documented as an ambigram pair, this transformation works only with:
1. Specially designed ambigram fonts
2. Stylized handwriting where 'a' is drawn to resemble 'e' when inverted
3. Lowercase letters in certain fonts (not standard block capitals)

In standard block capital letters (SAIL), a 180-degree rotation does not produce LIES or any other readable word.

**The distinction matters:**
- 180-degree rotation (upside down): SAIL -> unreadable/TIVS
- Horizontal mirror reflection: SAIL -> could produce LIES in specially designed fonts
