## Reasoning
The riddle describes something that is "full of holes" yet "doesn't hold any water." This is a classic riddle with a well-known answer. Let me think through the clues:

1. "Full of holes" - the object has many holes in it
2. "Doesn't hold any water" - despite having holes, it's not something that would retain water (or the phrase could be a play on the idiom "doesn't hold water," meaning doesn't make sense, but here it's literal)

Common answers to this riddle include:
- A sponge - but a sponge DOES hold water, so this doesn't fit
- A sieve/strainer - has holes, water passes through, doesn't hold water. This fits well.
- A net - full of holes, doesn't hold water (water passes through)
- Swiss cheese - full of holes, doesn't hold water

The most traditional and classic answer to this riddle is a sieve (or a net). A sieve is literally designed with holes and its purpose is that water (or fine particles) pass through it without being held.

## Final Answer
A sieve (or a net).
