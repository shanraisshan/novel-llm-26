## Reasoning
This is a classic riddle. Let me think through it:
- "Full of holes" - has many holes
- "Doesn't hold any water" - water passes right through it, or it fails to retain water

Common candidates for things full of holes:
- A sponge - full of holes but DOES hold water, so no
- A net - full of holes and doesn't hold water (water passes through the mesh)
- A sieve/strainer - full of holes and doesn't hold water
- Swiss cheese - has holes but isn't typically described this way in riddles

The most classic answer to this riddle is "a sponge," but a sponge actually DOES hold water, which contradicts the riddle. So that rules out sponge.

The answer that fits best is a net (or sieve). A net is full of holes and literally cannot hold water - the water passes straight through it.

There's also a figurative interpretation: "Your argument is full of holes and doesn't hold water" - but the riddle is asking for a physical object.

Between net and sieve, "a net" is the most common answer to this specific phrasing of the riddle.

## Final Answer
A net (or a sieve/strainer). A net is full of holes, and water passes right through it, so it cannot hold any water.
