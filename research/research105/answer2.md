## Reasoning
This is a classic riddle. Let me break down the clues:
1. "Full of holes" - the object has many holes in it
2. "Doesn't hold any water" - despite having holes, it cannot retain water (or water passes through it easily)

Common answers to "full of holes" riddles include:
- A sponge - but a sponge DOES hold water, so this is ruled out
- A sieve/strainer - full of holes, water passes right through it, doesn't hold water
- A net - full of holes, doesn't hold water
- Swiss cheese - full of holes, doesn't hold water

The most classic and well-known answer to this specific riddle is "a sieve" (or strainer). A sieve is literally designed with holes and its defining characteristic is that water passes through it without being held.

A net is also a strong answer - nets are full of holes and water passes through them.

The traditional/canonical answer to this riddle is a sieve.

## Final Answer
A sieve (a strainer). It is full of holes by design, yet water passes straight through it without being held.
