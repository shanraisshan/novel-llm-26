## Reasoning
The riddle describes something that is "full of holes" but "doesn't hold any water." This is a classic riddle with a well-known answer.

Let me think through the clues:
1. "Full of holes" - the object has many holes in it
2. "Doesn't hold any water" - despite being full of holes, it doesn't retain water (which is paradoxical because things with holes typically leak water)

Common candidates for this riddle:
- A sponge: has holes, but DOES hold water, so this doesn't fit
- A sieve/strainer: has holes and water passes through it, so it doesn't hold water - this fits
- A net: has holes, doesn't hold water - this fits
- Swiss cheese: has holes, doesn't hold water - but the "doesn't hold water" phrase is often idiomatic

The classic answer to this riddle is typically "a sieve" (or sometimes "a net"). The phrase "doesn't hold water" is also an idiom meaning "doesn't make sense" or "isn't valid," but in the literal sense, a sieve is designed with holes specifically so water passes through it rather than being held.

The most common and classic answer to this riddle is a sieve (or a net).

## Final Answer
A sieve (or a net) - it's full of holes, and water passes right through it rather than being held.
