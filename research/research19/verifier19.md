## Final Synthesized Answer

**The ball hits the ground first.**

In a living room environment with normal atmospheric conditions, air resistance plays a critical role in determining how quickly objects fall. While gravity accelerates both objects equally (approximately 9.8 m/s^2), the feather experiences dramatically more air resistance relative to its weight than the ball does.

The feather has a very large surface area compared to its tiny mass, causing it to quickly reach a slow terminal velocity and float/drift gently downward. The ball, being denser and more compact with a favorable mass-to-surface-area ratio, experiences minimal air resistance and falls quickly to the ground.

This is why in a vacuum (such as on the Moon, as demonstrated by Apollo 15 astronaut David Scott), a ball and feather would hit the ground at exactly the same time - without air, there is no resistance to differentiate their fall rates.
